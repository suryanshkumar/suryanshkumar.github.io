Résumé (updated April 2019)
suryansh.kumar at anu dot edu dot au
     

I am a Ph.D. student at the Australian National University Computer Vision Lab, where I am supervised by Yuchao Dai and Hongdong Li. I recently had a successful collaboration with Anoop Cherian. My research focuses on 3D reconstruction of a general dynamic scene and deformable objects from images. The approaches we proposed are now considered as the benchmark in 3D reconstruction of deformable objects.

I received M.S in Computer Science and Engineering from IIIT-Hyderabad, where I also worked as a research assistant in the Robotics Research Center (RRC). In the past, I was a Visiting Scientist at e-Motion Group, INRIA Grenoble, where I contributed to the autonomous driving project.

Research Interests

Geometric Computer Vision, Mathematical Optimization, Abstract Algebra and Machine Learning (Deep Neural Networks). From time to time, I also investigate topics such as graph theory, topological manifolds and compressed sensing.
