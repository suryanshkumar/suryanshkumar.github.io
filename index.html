I am a computer science researcher primarily focused in the areas of 3D computer vision. Presently, I hold the position of Professorship for Computer Vision in the Dept. of Information Technology and Electrical Engineering at ETH Zürich, where I am advised by Prof. Dr. Luc Van Gool. I received Ph.D. in Engineering and Computer Science from Australian National University on the topic Non-Rigid Structure from Motion. As a researcher, I am fascinated by how mathematical construction can precisely represent the perceptual concepts of images such as 3D scene geometry, motions, lights and color. Therefore, I aim to use these mathematical concepts to enable machines to experience and perceive the 3D world as humans. Presently, I am working on the projects that are supported by ETH Zürich Foundation and Google.

Besides my work life, I like to watch movies, musicals, learn to cook food, photograhy and, love to travel new places.

Research Interests

Geometric Computer Vision, Mathematical Optimization, Abstract Algebra and Geometric AI. From time to time, I also investigate topics such as graph theory, topological manifolds and compressed sensing.
