I recently received Ph.D. degree from Australian National University Computer Vision Lab on the topic Non-Rigid Structure from Motion. As a researcher, I am fascinated by how mathematical construction can precisely represent the perceptual concepts of images such as 3D scene geometry, motions, lights and color. Therefore, I aim to use these mathematical concepts to enable machines to experience and perceive the 3D world as humans. Presently, I am researching the field of 3D shape correspondence for non-rigidly deforming subjects. My past research problem was focused on 3D reconstruction of a general dynamic scene and deformable objects from images. The approaches we proposed in the past is now considered as the benchmark in 3D reconstruction of deformable objects. During my Ph.D. I was supervised by an extraordinary computer vision group led by Hongdong Li

I received M.S in Computer Science and Engineering from IIIT-Hyderabad, where I also worked as a research assistant in the Robotics Research Center (RRC). In the past, I was a Visiting Scientist at e-Motion Group, INRIA Grenoble, where I contributed to the autonomous driving project.

Besides my work life, I like to watch movies, listens to music, learn to cook food, photograhy and love to travel new places.

Research Interests

Geometric Computer Vision, Mathematical Optimization, Abstract Algebra and Geometric AI. From time to time, I also investigate topics such as graph theory, topological manifolds and compressed sensing.
